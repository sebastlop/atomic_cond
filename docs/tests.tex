\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

We use a neural network as universal interpolator to 
fit calculations of total cross sections (TCS) within two theoretical 
models, namely the Continuum-Distorted-Wave with Eikonal-Initial
-State (CDW-EIS) and the Classical Trajectory MonteCarlo (CTMC)
for ionization of hydrogen by bare ion impact. Here we consider
bare projectiles with charges $Z_P$ ranging between 1 and 9 

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsubsection{The training set}

calculated $ 513 $ TCS with the CDW-EIS model and the CTMC model 
as it is specified in Table \ref{Table-TS}. It is important to 
note that we have considered here the semiempiric model of Rudd

Since MLP are not suitable to handle very tiny numbers nor 
several orders of magnitude, as in the case of this 
training set, we must perform a preprocessing stage that 
include the data compression by taking the logarithm of the 
impact energy and the  
TCS. On the other hand, the categorical variable corresponding 
to the theory must be casted to a numerical value. We choose 
to expand a three dimensional Onem Hot encoding for the three 
theories considered here. These preprocessing allow us to 
avoid gradient vanishing in the backpropagation algorithm 
used to fit the data because using tiny floating point 
values for the energy and the TCS. Moreover, logarithm produce
a compactacion of the several magnitude orders that TCS 
present for the wide impact energies considered here.  

\subsection{The model}
To choice the architecture of the MLP we have performed 
k-folded trainings over the training set for models with 
hidden layers ranging from one to five, combining different 
number of units per layer and activation functions. We noted that 
increasing the number of layers become meaningless after a total 
of three layers. On the other hand the use of different number 
of units in the different layers did not improve the fitting, 
for these reasons we choose to employ a MLP with three hidden 
layers and present the study of the training performance for 
the case with 32, 64 and 128 units per layer.
As it is well known, to introduce non linearity in the MLPs, 
some non linear activation functions must be used for each unit 
output. Unlike the normal trend in of using the fast-to-compute 
Rectifier Linear Unit activation (ReLU), in this work we use 
two kind of infinite differentiable functions: the hyperbolic 
tangent, and a gaussian activation. The choice of smooth 
activation functions can be easily justified by the fact that 
TCS are smooth functions of the impact energy, and extreme 
non differentiable oscillating fits caused by ReLU and its 
variations are naturally avoided.

\begin{figure}[htbp]

    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.8\textwidth]{./Esquema_ionNN.pdf}
    \caption{Interpolator scheme}
    \label{fig:scheme}
\end{figure}

We show in Figure \ref{fig:scheme} a scheme of the model used 
in this work. After the preprocessing stage we have a 
5-dimensional input, three hidden layers and a single output 
corresponding to the $\log_{10}$ of the input energy.

We use PyTorch framework to optimize the fitting of a multilayer 
perceptron (MLP) over a set of pre-calculated ionization TCS.

The architecture of the MLP has an input of the projectile impact 
energy, the charge of the projectile and the theory used

\begin{itemize}
    \item Impact energy: we use decimal logarithm to flat the data in 
    usable ranges $\approx 10$
    \item Projectile charge: casted to float
    \item Theory: we one-hot encode the theory used (CTMC, CDW-EIS and 
    semi-empirical formula for proton impact by Rudd et al.)
\end{itemize}
